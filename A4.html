North Georgetown secondary school was opened on September 15, 1969. However, the formal session began on September 22, 1969 when sanitary facilities became available.


One hundred and sixty three students, including the students of south Georgetown secondary, which was still under construction, were enrolled at this new school.


These students were housed in six classrooms. There were also 13 members of staff, which comprised five males, eight females, including the headmaster, Mr. Victor Ralph. Apart from the academic studies such as English, social studies, mathematics, science, home economics and technical drawing, the staff and students were often involved in community activities.


When the school reassembled on September 1970 for the second year, a large number of students were enrolled. One month later the school first inter-house athletic meeting was held.


On April 30th 1971, there was a formal opening of the school. The main address was delivered by Mr. Fox who was the chief education officer at that time.

The original school building has been extended to accommodate food and nutrition, building technology and information technology departments. The school presently has on its enrollment approximately 42 staff members and 533 students. Making North Georgetown Secondary a grade A school.
